Quick answer

For most people, the Marcato Atlas 150 is the gold standard, simple, durable, and capable of professional-quality pasta right on your countertop.

If you prefer hands-off convenience, the Philips automatic machine saves kneading and rolling; for KitchenAid owners, the roller attachment is a no-brainer upgrade that uses the mixer you already own.

Whether you are a weekend ravioli obsessive or just tired of flimsy grocery-store dried pasta, choosing the right machine can feel surprisingly tricky. Manual models are beloved for control and longevity but require arm work. Automatics promise effortless sheets and shapes but you will pay more and may sacrifice dough feel. This site cuts through the noise with honest, researched picks, no gimmicks, just the machines that earn their keep in real kitchens.

How we choose our picks

We do not run a test kitchen or time laps in a lab. Our picks are based on the long-term reputation of each model, years of owner feedback from forums, customer reviews, and attention to what actually breaks or annoys people after months of use. A machine that collects dust because cleaning is a hassle is not a good machine, no matter how glossy its marketing. For manual machines, we look at gear durability, smoothness of the crank action, and availability of replacement parts. For automatics and attachments, we weigh motor strain, ease of cleaning, and whether the pasta comes out at a consistent thickness. Extrusion-style machines like the Philips get extra scrutiny for clogging issues and drying time. The final picks are machines that have earned a steady following from hungry home cooks who keep coming back to them.
